Project Overview

The project focused on developing innovative fixation concepts for busbars in the Power Distribution Unit (PDU) of commercial electric vehicles. The goal was to improve mechanical robustness, thermal/electrical safety, and assembly efficiency while meeting strict automotive requirements for reliability and cost-effectiveness.

Problem Statement

The existing fixation methods for busbars in Volvo‚Äôs Traction Voltage Power Distribution Center (TVPDC) had several mechanical and electrical limitations, along with challenges in assembly accessibility, packaging efficiency, and long-term durability. A new solution was required to ensure safety, manufacturability, and compliance with high-voltage standards.

My Role & Responsibilities

  • Conducted research on current busbar fixation design, electrical insulation, mechanical strength, and failure modes.
  • Led concept generation and selection using systematic product development methods: Function-Means Tree, Pugh Matrix, Morphological Matrix.
  • Performed material selection using Granta Edupack, focusing on insulation and clamping materials.
  • Developed 3D CAD models in Creo Parametric and performed structural simulations in ANSYS Mechanical (tear-out and shear strength).
  • Collaborated with Volvo Electromobility engineers to align concepts with real-world constraints.

Approach & Method

  • Knowledge gathering: Interviews with Volvo engineers, benchmarking (e.g., Mercedes E-Actros), patent research, and requirements definition.
  • Concept development: Brainstormed, classified, and iteratively screened multiple fixation concepts.
  • Engineering analysis: Verified load-bearing and insulation requirements through CAE simulations.
  • Evaluation: Ranked concepts based on manufacturability, assembly time, cost, and performance.

Results and Outcomes

  • Generated 10+ initial concepts, narrowed down to 3 final candidates.
  • Final fixation design ensured:
    • Improved assembly efficiency (reduced fixation points & complexity).
    • Compliance with electrical safety (creepage & clearance standards).
    • Material optimization using glass-fiber reinforced polymers for insulation & strength.
    • 40% reduction in stress concentrations in CAE simulations vs baseline.
  • Delivered a design catalogue of fixation solutions for future PDU iterations.
