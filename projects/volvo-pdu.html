Busbar Fixation for Commercial EV’s PDU
Volvo Trucks · Electromobility

Master’s thesis focused on developing innovative fixation concepts for high-voltage busbars in Volvo’s traction voltage PDU to improve mechanical robustness, electrical/thermal safety, and assembly efficiency.

Background

The project focused on developing innovative fixation concepts for busbars in the Power Distribution Unit (PDU) of commercial electric vehicles. The goal was to improve mechanical robustness, thermal/electrical safety, and assembly efficiency while meeting strict automotive requirements for reliability and cost-effectiveness.

Existing fixation methods for busbars in Volvo’s Traction Voltage Power Distribution Center (TVPDC) had several mechanical and electrical limitations along with problems related to assembly accessibility, packaging efficiency, and long-term durability. A new solution was required to ensure safety, manufacturability, and compliance with high-voltage standards.

Objective

The work aimed to:

  • Develop alternative fixation concepts for HV busbars inside the PDU.
  • Increase structural robustness and vibration resistance.
  • Improve electrical insulation, creepage, and clearance.
  • Enhance assembly accessibility and packaging efficiency.
  • Align with Volvo’s manufacturing and PLM workflows.

Approach & Method

The thesis followed the Systematic Product Development methodology (Ulrich & Eppinger) to move from problem identification to CAD- and CAE-backed solutions:

  1. Current-state analysis: Studied existing PDU and busbar integration to identify pain points.
  2. Requirements: Collected mechanical, electrical, and assembly requirements from Volvo electromobility guidelines and HV standards.
  3. Concept generation: Created multiple fixation concepts and performed structured selection using morphological and Pugh matrices.
  4. CAD modelling: Modelled shortlisted concepts in PTC Creo Parametric and prepared visualizations.
  5. CAE validation: Used ANSYS Mechanical to assess structural behavior, stresses, and mounting integrity.
  6. Material selection: Used Granta EduPack to select insulation and structural materials.
  7. PLM documentation: Documented the final design(s) in Windchill / Kola for future design iterations.

Tools & Technologies

Results & Outcomes

  • Identified key mechanical and electrical limitations in the existing PDU busbar fixation.
  • Proposed multiple fixation concepts that improved robustness, safety, and assembly accessibility.
  • Validated promising concepts through FEM-based structural analysis and material screening.
  • Delivered solutions aligned with high-voltage safety standards and manufacturability needs.
  • Work supported a patent application with Volvo Trucks (pending).
