Chalmers Formula Student – Chassis & Ergonomics

Chalmers Formula Student (CFS) is a multidisciplinary motorsport engineering project where students design, manufacture, and race a Formula-style electric and autonomous race car. In 2024, the team developed Freja, designed and built ~97% in-house by 70+ students from 11 nationalities, supported by 50+ industrial partners, and competing in both DV and EV classes.

My work focused on the carbon fiber monocoque chassis, safety structures, and ergonomics, ensuring compliance with Formula Student rules while enabling integration of suspension, aerodynamics, high-voltage, and driverless systems.

Background

CFS 2024 (Freja) was designed to compete in both Electric Vehicle (EV) and Driverless Vehicle (DV) classes at Formula Student East (Hungary) and Formula Student Germany (Hockenheim). The challenge on the chassis/safety side was to create a lightweight yet structurally safe carbon fiber monocoque that complied with Formula Student rules (crash, egress, harness, roll structures) while providing interfaces for EV components, suspension pickups, aero hardpoints, and DV sensors.

The chassis team had to work closely with Aerodynamics, Suspension, Vehicle Dynamics, Electrical, and Driverless subteams to ensure all packaging and load paths were supported in the monocoque design.

My Role

  • Worked on the carbon fiber monocoque CAD and layup definition.
  • Ensured compliance with FS rules for impact attenuator, front bulkhead, side impact, and driver safety.
  • Coordinated chassis interfaces with aero, suspension, and electrical teams.
  • Prepared documentation (SES/IAD) in Microsoft Excel and Teamcenter.

Chassis & Safety Design

The monocoque was designed as a lightweight CFRP safety cell with integrated hardpoints for suspension and aero. The design had to meet minimum laminate thicknesses, crash energy absorption targets, and ergonomic constraints for the driver.

Simulation and validation were carried out to ensure adequate bending and torsional stiffness, load transfer to A-arm pickups, and compliance of the impact attenuator with FS requirements.

  • Monocoque CAD in Siemens NX / CATIA V5
  • FEA and crash analysis in ANSYS
  • Composite simulation in ANSYS ACP
  • Layup nesting and documentation

EV & Driverless Integration

Because Freja ran in both EV and DV classes, the chassis had to provide mounting and protection for:

  • HV battery and power electronics
  • Wiring and sensor harness routing
  • Driverless sensors (LiDAR, cameras, GPS) and their structural mounts
  • Serviceability and access for scrutineering

This required close coordination with the DV team to ensure sensor visibility and with the electrical team to maintain insulation and clearance, without compromising driver ergonomics.

Competition & Outcomes

  • Freja was designed and built ~97% in-house by a 70+ member team.
  • The car competed in both EV and Driverless classes at FS East and FSG.
  • Chassis met stiffness, safety, and manufacturability requirements while enabling integration of other subsystems.
  • Documentation and CAD were aligned in Teamcenter for cross-team access.
