Overview

This project focused on the aerostructural performance validation of a carbon fiber composite rear wing for a Formula Student race car. The goal was to perform a one-way fluid‚Äìstructure interaction (FSI) simulation to assess the deformation, stress, and safety compliance of the aerodynamic assembly under racing conditions.

Problem Statement

In high-downforce racing environments, aerodynamic loads can induce significant deformation in composite components, affecting both aerodynamic efficiency and vehicle safety. The objective was to accurately simulate the coupling between aerodynamic pressure loads and structural response, ensuring the wing‚Äôs stiffness, strength, and rule compliance.

My Role & Responsibilities

  • Responsible for FEA‚ÄìCFD coupling workflow, integrating ANSYS Fluent pressure maps with ANSYS ACP and ANSYS Mechanical for one-way FSI simulation.
  • Verified accurate pressure mapping onto the composite mesh and defined boundary conditions for fixed supports.
  • Performed deformation and stress analysis for multiple angles of attack to evaluate load sensitivity and safety factors.
  • Assisted in aerodynamic simulations using STAR-CCM+ and ANSYS Fluent, validating flow characteristics and downforce values.

Approach & Method

  • Simulated aerodynamic flow at 100 km/h using the k‚Äìœâ SST turbulence model to generate pressure distributions.

  • Imported aerodynamic pressure fields from Fluent into Static Structural for FEA deformation and stress analysis.
  • Iteratively refined mesh quality, coupling surfaces, and support constraints to ensure accuracy.

Key Results & Outcomes

  • Achieved 954 N downforce at 100 km/h, consistent across STAR-CCM+ and Fluent.
