Overview

Served as a teaching assistant and lab instructor for Vehicle Motion Engineering (MMF062) and Road Vehicle Aerodynamics (MTF236). Delivered simulator-based sessions with Chalmers’ CASTER full-scale driving simulator, supporting students in applying theoretical models of vehicle dynamics and aerodynamics to hands-on experiments and simulations.

Problem Statement

Students were required to validate theoretical models of vehicle motion and aerodynamics through simulation and lab experiments. Key challenges included understanding tire slip behavior, brake bias dynamics, steering response, steady-state cornering, and aerodynamic downforce effects. Guided support was crucial to help bridge theory and real-world behavior.

My Role & Responsibilities

  • Facilitated lab sessions using MATLAB/Simulink and the CASTER simulator.
  • Guided students in implementing and validating models for:
    • Steering dynamics and longitudinal slip behaviour using the Magic Formula tire model and free-body diagrams.
    • Brake bias distribution and its effect on stability under varying axle loads.
    • Steady-state cornering, lateral acceleration, and slip angle behaviour.
  • Led aerodynamics labs demonstrating how downforce and aero packages influence cornering stability, maximum lateral acceleration, and handling.
  • Supervised data analysis & visualization, ensuring students could compare simulator outputs with theoretical predictions and simulation plots.

Approach / Process

  • Linked longitudinal and lateral dynamics equations with simulation tasks, allowing students to test their models on the CASTER simulator for practical understanding.
  • Demonstrated how to implement slip models, tire force computations, and power consumption analysis in MATLAB.
  • Used CASTER simulator experiments to compare vehicle behaviour with and without aerodynamic aids, highlighting measurable differences in cornering forces, velocity, and lap time.
  • Encouraged rigorous evaluation by comparing simulated vs theoretical results to help students understand real-world limitations.

Key Results & Outcomes

  • Improved student proficiency in validating vehicle models and interpreting simulation data.
  • Enabled students to clearly see how tire dynamics, brake bias, and aerodynamics influence vehicle stability and performance.
  • Contributed to better learning outcomes by making abstract concepts significantly more intuitive and application-oriented.

Tools & Technologies

  • MATLAB & Simulink
  • CASTER full-scale driving simulator
  • Magic Formula tire model & slip dynamics
  • Aerodynamic models (CL, downforce, lateral acceleration)

Learnings & Impact

  • Reinforced expertise in vehicle motion dynamics and aerodynamics through teaching.
  • Developed strong communication skills for explaining complex engineering concepts.
  • Helped graduate-level students connect theory → simulation → real vehicle behaviour.
