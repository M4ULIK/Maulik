Teaching Assistant & Lab Instructor – Chalmers University

Assisted in delivering master’s-level courses in vehicle dynamics and road vehicle aerodynamics, supporting simulator-based labs using the CASTER full-scale driving simulator and MATLAB/Simulink-based exercises.

The work involved guiding students through modelling, parameter tuning, and validation tasks so that they could link classroom theory with real vehicle behavior.

Courses Supported

  • Vehicle Motion Engineering – dynamics, stability, and control of road vehicles.
  • Road Vehicle Aerodynamics – aerodynamic forces, drag reduction, and flow visualization.

Labs were designed to help students connect theoretical vehicle dynamics/aero content to measurable parameters in a controlled environment.

Lab Setup & Teaching Approach

The core of the exercises was the CASTER full-scale driving simulator, which allows students to experience vehicle behavior in real time and compare it with model predictions. MATLAB/Simulink models and prepared scenarios were used to demonstrate the effect of vehicle parameters on stability, cornering, and aerodynamic loads.

  • Prepared simulator scenario and configuration.
  • Guided students through running experiments.
  • Helped them post-process results in MATLAB/Excel.
  • Ensured safety and correct use of the simulator.

Responsibilities

  • Explained lab objectives and related theory at the start of each session.
  • Helped students implement or modify MATLAB/Simulink models.
  • Troubleshot issues related to simulator setup or signal acquisition.
  • Reviewed student lab reports and clarified evaluation criteria.
  • Provided 1:1 guidance to groups working on vehicle dynamics or aero-related assignments.

Tools & Software

  • MATLAB & Simulink – data processing, model-based labs.
  • CASTER driving simulator – full-scale, used for vehicle motion experiments.
  • Excel – post-processing and lab report templates.
  • Course LMS / documentation – distributing assignments and lab material.

Impact & Learnings

  • Improved student understanding of how theoretical models map to real vehicle behavior.
  • Strengthened communication and technical mentoring skills.
  • Gained experience in operating and maintaining a full-scale driving simulator for teaching.
  • Supported course staff in delivering high-quality lab experiences.
