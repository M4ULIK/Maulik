Vehicle Dynamics & Motion Control – Volvo Group Trucks Technology

Development of a kinematic control logic for long combination vehicles (LCVs) to estimate the pose of trailing units and reduce off-tracking and jackknifing, using limited signal availability over the ISO 11992 communication standard.

The work supported Volvo’s research on safer and more maneuverable multi-unit commercial vehicles operating in constrained environments.

Background

Long combination vehicles (LCVs) and multi-unit trucks offer improved freight efficiency, but they are more prone to off-tracking, tail swing, and jackknifing, especially at low speeds, in roundabouts, or during docking maneuvers.

Modern trucks communicate over standards such as ISO 11992, but the available signals from the trailer units can be limited or delayed. This creates a challenge for estimating the pose (position and orientation) of the rear unit in real time and for applying corrective control actions.

Problem / Challenge

The project addressed two main issues:

  • Limited signal availability: not all trailer states (angles, yaw rate, articulation) are directly measured or sent over ISO 11992, so the system must infer or estimate them.
  • Need for kinematic consistency: the pose of the trailing unit must be consistent with the mechanical connection and the geometry of the vehicle train.

The control logic therefore needed to be robust to missing or delayed signals and able to reconstruct trailer pose from a minimal signal set.

Control Approach

The solution was based on a kinematic modelling approach for multi-unit vehicles, where the motion of the trailing unit is derived from the motion of the tractor and the geometry of the articulation points.

  1. System definition: defined the vehicle train configuration, articulation points, and available ISO 11992 signals.
  2. Kinematic model: formulated vehicle-train kinematics to relate tractor motion to trailer pose.
  3. Signal handling: handled missing/limited signals from the trailer by using kinematic reconstruction.
  4. Control logic: implemented logic to reduce off-tracking and prevent large articulation angles.
  5. Simulation: tested the strategy in a vehicle-dynamics environment representative of Volvo tools.

This approach made it possible to estimate trailer position and heading in real time without requiring a full sensor suite on every trailer.

Tools & Models

  • MATLAB / Simulink for control logic implementation and rapid prototyping.
  • Vehicle-dynamics environment (Volvo) for multi-unit vehicle simulation and scenario testing.
  • ISO 11992 signal structure as a constraint for available data.
  • Supporting documentation prepared in standard Volvo formats.

Results & Impact

  • Demonstrated that trailer pose can be reconstructed from limited ISO 11992 signals using kinematic relations.
  • Enabled control actions aimed at reducing off-tracking and avoiding jackknifing in low-speed maneuvers.
  • Provided a basis for future integration into advanced driver assistance or automated yard operations.
  • Documented assumptions, limitations, and signal requirements for further development within Volvo GTT.
