Project Overview

This internship was part of Volvo’s long-term research into improving the safety and maneuverability of Long Combination Vehicles (LCVs), such as A-double truck configurations. The project focused on developing a control logic to mitigate off-tracking and prevent jackknifing, both critical challenges in heavy multi-trailer operations.

Problem Statement

LCVs face severe stability and control issues during complex maneuvers, particularly reversing, due to limited sensors on trailing units. The challenge was to estimate the position and orientation of all trailing units using only truck-mounted IMU and ISO 11992-2/3 signals, enabling safe real-time control without additional hardware.

My Role & Responsibilities

  • Developed a kinematic model–based control logic to estimate the position, slope, and articulation angles of trailing units in real time, despite sensor limitations.
  • Designed a trajectory memory system that stored 100 m of vehicle path data, enabling accurate reversing and reducing jackknifing risk.
  • Investigated usage of ISO 11992-2/3 signals and identified the need for additional signals to support advanced motion control.
  • Worked under a lumped mass model assumption with all-wheel steered and powered axles as a future implementation scenario.

Approach & Methods

  • Vehicle modelling: Modelled the A-double vehicle combination using vehicle dynamics and LCV kinematics.
  • State estimation: Implemented logic for real-time X–Y coordinate and slope estimation of each unit relative to the lead truck.
  • Control logic: Implemented longitudinal and yaw control logic in MATLAB/Simulink as a future extension of the model.
  • Model validation: Validated the model by:
    • Comparing predictions with real-world CAN signal data at various angles and velocities.
    • Benchmarking against Volvo’s in-house simulation tool VTM with track-based test scenarios.
  • Off-tracking analysis: Developed visualizations of tractor and trailer paths to calculate off-tracking distances, providing insights for correction values in steering and velocity control.

Key Results & Outcomes

  • Built a working real-time kinematic control logic capable of tracking trailing unit positions without requiring onboard IMUs.
  • Delivered a trajectory memory–based reversal strategy to minimize jackknifing during reversing.
  • Identified critical signal limitations in ISO 11992-2/3, and recommended extensions for future LCV motion control systems.
  • Delivered tools for visualizing articulation and off-tracking, aiding the design of corrective control strategies.

Tools & Technologies

  • MATLAB & Simulink
  • Vehicle dynamics modelling – LCV kinematics, lumped mass approach
  • ISO 11992-2/3 communication standard
  • Volvo VTM – in-house vehicle dynamics and maneuvering simulation tool

Learnings & Impact

  • Strengthened expertise in control system design for long combination vehicles.
  • Learned to validate models by bridging simulation results with real-world CAN signal data.
  • Gained insights into off-tracking dynamics and corrective control strategies.
  • Contributed to Volvo’s research roadmap for safer LCV operation and future yaw/steering control solutions.
