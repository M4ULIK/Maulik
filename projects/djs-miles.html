Overview / Context

DJS Miles is the official Electric Supermileage Vehicle team of Dwarkadas J. Sanghvi College of Engineering, founded during the pandemic with the goal of building India‚Äôs first student-developed ultra-efficient electric vehicle.

The team competed in the Shell Eco-Marathon Asia Pacific & Middle East (APME) region for two consecutive seasons (2020‚Äì2021, 2021‚Äì2022), representing India among 250+ international teams.

Problem Statement

The key challenge was to conceptualize and develop an electric vehicle optimised for maximum energy efficiency, while remaining manufacturable by a student team.

Engineering focus areas included an aerodynamic teardrop carbon fiber monocoque, lightweight structural design, highly efficient three-wheeled steering layout, and tight packaging of the EV drivetrain in a minimal envelope.

My Role & Responsibilities

  • Co-founded the team and later led a 25-member interdisciplinary group through the 2021‚Äì2022 season.
  • Worked as Vehicle Dynamics & Structural Engineer, responsible for:
    • Vehicle Dynamics: designed and optimised steering geometry and upright assembly for a three-wheeled electric prototype to achieve maximum stability, minimum scrub radius, and precise Ackermann geometry.
    • Structural Engineering: designed and manufactured the teardrop carbon fiber sandwich composite monocoque, ensuring stiffness, low mass, and manufacturability via vacuum bagging.
  • Led off-track and static competitions such as Vehicle Design, Future Rider, and Pitch the Future, focusing on innovation and sustainability.
  • Managed sponsorship and technical communication with universities and industry partners in India and the USA.

Approach

  • Adopted a simulation-driven design process: vehicle modelling, CAD, analysis, fabrication, and test-based validation.
  • Integrated composite manufacturing, additive manufacturing, and topology optimisation to reduce weight while maintaining structural integrity.
  • Performed iterative CFD and FEA to refine aerodynamic performance and component strength.
  • Organised material panel tests and full-vehicle efficiency trials to verify performance against Shell Eco-Marathon targets.

Steering System & Upright Design
