Background

Shell Eco-Marathon challenges student teams to design and build ultra energy-efficient vehicles. Our goal was to create an electric, 3-wheeled, teardrop-shaped composite vehicle with minimal drag and mass, while still being manufacturable with the resources available to a student team.

The project demanded concurrent work on aerodynamics, lightweight structure, driveline packaging, and steering kinematics, with strong emphasis on documentation for the competition.

My Role

  • Co-founded and led the DJS Miles team.
  • Drove the overall mechanical concept: 3-wheeled EV with composite monocoque.
  • Designed the outer body and structural shell for minimum drag and weight.
  • Coordinated manufacturing and integration for the competition vehicle.
  • Prepared technical documentation for off-track awards.

Vehicle & Structural Design

The vehicle used a teardrop-shaped carbon fiber monocoque to achieve low frontal area and smooth flow. The composite shell acted as both the aerodynamic surface and the primary structure, reducing part count and weight.

  • 3-wheeled layout for reduced rolling resistance.
  • Carbon fiber / composite monocoque for stiffness-to-weight.
  • Body optimized for smooth airflow over canopy and tail.

Steering, Driver, and EV Packaging

Because of the narrow, aerodynamic body, integrating steering, driver ergonomics, and the EV powertrain in a limited space was a key challenge.

  • Steering system designed to fit the narrow nose profile.
  • Driver position optimized for visibility and low CdA.
  • Battery and motor packaging arranged for serviceability and mass centralization.

The final layout balanced manufacturability with competition requirements.

Tools & Technologies

  • Fusion 360 / SolidWorks – CAD modelling of body, chassis, and steering.
  • ANSYS – structural checks on composite shell.
  • Excel / documentation – competition reports and design justification.
