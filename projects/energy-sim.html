Energy Performance Simulation of Heavy-Duty BEV and ICE Trucks

Developed a forward vehicle simulation framework in MATLAB/Simulink to evaluate the energy and cost performance of heavy-duty battery-electric (BEV) and internal combustion engine (ICE) freight vehicles operating on real Swedish transport routes.

The work supported ongoing research on freight electrification strategies for Volvo Group Trucks Technology (GTT) and ReVeRe.

Background

Electrifying heavy-duty freight transport is challenging because energy demand depends strongly on route profile, payload, speed profile, and environmental conditions. Operators need to understand whether a BEV truck can complete a given route, what the energy cost will be, and how it compares to an equivalent ICE truck.

The project set out to create a simulation environment where different powertrain concepts could be tested on real operating routes used by Volvo GTT and ReVeRe.

Objective

  • Build a forward vehicle simulation model for HD trucks.
  • Simulate both BEV and ICE powertrains under comparable conditions.
  • Use real Swedish freight routes (topology, speed limits, distance).
  • Compare energy consumption and cost implications.
  • Provide a tool to support freight electrification decision-making.

Simulation Method

A forward-facing vehicle model was implemented in MATLAB/Simulink, where driver demand and route conditions are inputs, and the model computes tractive effort, powertrain operation, and resulting energy use.

  1. Vehicle model: modelled longitudinal dynamics of HD vehicles (mass, rolling resistance, aero drag, road grade).
  2. Powertrain model: separate branches for BEV (motor + battery + efficiency maps) and ICE (engine + gearbox + fuel maps).
  3. Driver / speed profile: tracked the target speed from route data.
  4. Environment: included effects of road topology and possibly ambient conditions.
  5. Post-processing: energy/fuel use aggregated per trip for cost comparison.

The structure allowed swapping powertrains while keeping the same route and mission profile.

Route & Data

The model used Volvo GTT freight route data from Sweden to reflect actual operating conditions (distance, elevation, speed limits). This was important to assess BEV feasibility on specific transport corridors.

  • Realistic distance and road grade.
  • Speed and stop patterns as per route.
  • Used for both BEV and ICE cases for apples-to-apples comparison.

Results & Insights

  • Energy consumption for BEV could be quantified per route and compared to ICE fuel consumption.
  • Cost performance (energy price vs fuel price) could be assessed for Swedish conditions.
  • Model highlighted routes where BEV range or charging might become a constraint.
  • Showed the value of using real route data instead of generic drive cycles.

These results can support planning of BEV deployment, charging strategy, and TCO analysis in heavy-duty applications.

Tools & Technologies

  • MATLAB – model logic and data processing.
  • Simulink – forward vehicle simulation environment.
  • Excel – post-processing and comparison tables.
  • Volvo GTT route data – real transport routes in Sweden.
