About Me

I am a Mechanical Engineer and a Master’s graduate in Mobility Engineering (Automotive Track) from Chalmers University of Technology, with a strong focus on mechanical design, structural mechanics, simulations, and optimization. My academic and project experiences span across automotive, motorsport, and sustainable mobility, where I’ve worked on everything from lightweight composite structures and aerodynamics to vehicle dynamics, electric powertrain systems, and high-voltage safety design.

Having contributed to award-winning projects in Formula Student and Shell Eco-Marathon, as well as industrial research at Volvo Group Trucks Technology, I bring a balance of hands-on engineering, simulation-driven problem solving, and innovation. I am motivated by the challenge of creating vehicles that are not only high-performing but also sustainable, and I enjoy applying an interdisciplinary approach that blends CAE, CAD, structural optimization, and system-level thinking.

My key interests lie in motorsport and automotive-inspired design, electric mobility, and performance-driven engineering solutions. My approach combines technical precision with creative problem-solving, always pushing the boundaries of what’s possible in mechanical engineering.
